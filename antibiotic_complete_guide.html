🧠 GP Quick Decision Rules — 70 kg Adult

Confirmed MSSA → Best Choice

  • Nafcillin or Oxacillin 2 g IV q4h (proven superior to Vancomycin)
  • Alternative: Cefazolin 2 g IV q8h (better tolerated)
  • De-escalate from Vancomycin immediately when MSSA confirmed

MRSA — Treatment Ladder

  • Bacteremia: Vancomycin 1,000–1,250 mg IV q12h (AUC guided)
  • Pneumonia: Linezolid 600 mg IV q12h (better lung penetration)
  • VRE/Dapto-fail: Linezolid 600 mg q12h or Daptomycin 700+ mg q24h
  • Salvage: Ceftaroline + Daptomycin combo

⚠️ Never Forget — GP

  • Daptomycin: NEVER in pneumonia (surfactant)
  • Vancomycin: infuse ≥60 min/g (Red Man prevention)
  • Linezolid + SSRIs: Serotonin Syndrome risk
  • MSSA confirmed: switch OFF Vancomycin to Oxacillin

✅ Empiric Hospital GP Sepsis (70 kg)

  • Vancomycin 1,000–1,250 mg IV q12h (AUC-guided)
  • De-escalate at 48–72h when culture sensitivity known
  • Add β-lactam if MSSA more likely (community-onset)

🧠 GN Quick Decision Rules — 70 kg Adult

ESBL → Carbapenem Choice

  • Meropenem 1–2 g q8h — gold standard for severe
  • Ertapenem 1 g q24h — ESBL UTI/abdominal, OPAT
  • Never rely on Pip-Tazo or Ceftriaxone for ESBL

Pseudomonas → Coverage Ladder

  • Step 1: Pip-Tazo 4.5 g q8h EI (4h) or Cefepime 2 g q8h EI (3h)
  • Step 2: Meropenem 2 g q8h EI (3h)
  • Step 3: Caz-Avi 2.5 g q8h
  • XDR: Colistin + Meropenem combination

⚠️ Never Forget — GN

  • Ertapenem: NO Pseudomonas (most dangerous omission)
  • Cefepime: reduce dose in renal impairment (NCSE risk)
  • Colistin: ALWAYS combine — monotherapy = resistance
  • Tigecycline: NEVER for bacteremia or UTI

✅ Empiric Severe GN Sepsis (ICU, 70 kg)

  • Meropenem 2 g IV q8h + Amikacin 1,200 mg IV q24h
  • Or Pip-Tazo 4.5 g q8h EI if low ESBL risk
  • De-escalate at 48–72h based on culture sensitivity

🧠 Empiric Sepsis Protocol — 70 kg Adult (2024 Surviving Sepsis Guidelines)

CAP / Community Sepsis

  • Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + Azithromycin 500 mg IV q24h (atypical cover)
  • Duration: 5–7 days (reassess at 48h)
  • Switch to oral when haemodynamically stable

HAP / VAP Empiric (Non-ESBL risk)

  • Pip-Tazo 4.5 g IV EI q8h + Vancomycin 1,000 mg IV q12h
  • De-escalate at 48h
  • If ESBL risk → Meropenem 2 g q8h instead

Septic Shock (Unknown source, ICU)

  • Meropenem 2 g IV q8h EI + Vancomycin 1,000–1,250 mg q12h (AUC)
  • Consider Antifungal if Candida risk
  • Cultures BEFORE antibiotics — but don't delay >1 hour

⚠️ Antibiotic Stewardship Rules

  • De-escalate at 48–72h when culture data available
  • Do NOT continue carbapenem beyond necessity
  • Total duration: 5–7 days most infections (not 10–14)
  • Short courses = less resistance, less toxicity, less C. diff

🧠 Pakistan Oral Antibiotic — Quick Reference by Infection Type

UTI (Uncomplicated, Women)

  • 1st: Nitrofurantoin 100 mg q12h × 5 days
  • 2nd: Fosfomycin 3 g single dose
  • 3rd: TMP-SMX DS 1 tab q12h × 3 days (if susceptible)
  • Avoid FQ unless culture-guided (resistance high)

CAP (Community Pneumonia)

  • Mild: Amoxicillin 1 g q8h × 5–7 days
  • Atypical suspected: + Azithromycin 500 mg q24h × 3–5 days
  • Moderate: Amoxiclav 625 mg q8h + Azithromycin 500 mg q24h
  • Duration: 5 days if responding well (2019 ATS/IDSA)

Typhoid Fever (Pakistan)

  • 1st (susceptible): Ciprofloxacin 500 mg q12h × 7–10 days
  • XDR Typhoid: Azithromycin 1 g q24h × 5–7 days (Pakistan strain)
  • Cefixime 400 mg q12h × 7–14 days (alternative)
  • Parenteral: Ceftriaxone 2 g q12h for severe/XDR

⚠️ Important — Pakistan Resistance

  • XDR Typhoid: resistant to all oral antibiotics except Azithromycin & FQ (some)
  • High FQ resistance in E. coli UTI — culture before FQ
  • High H. pylori Clarithromycin resistance — use Bismuth quadruple if triple fails
  • Always check local antibiogram before empiric oral therapy

⚡ Critical Interactions & Infusion Rules

Dangerous Drug Combinations

  • Linezolid + SSRIs/SNRIs → Serotonin Syndrome
  • Vancomycin + Pip-Tazo → ↑↑ Nephrotoxicity
  • Aminoglycosides + Loop diuretics → ↑ Ototoxicity
  • Nafcillin + Warfarin → ↓ INR (CYP3A4 induction)
  • Daptomycin + Statins → ↑ Myopathy (CPK↑)
  • Ciprofloxacin + Antacids/Iron → ↓ Absorption (chelation)

Infusion Rate Rules

  • Vancomycin: minimum 1h/gram (prevent Red Man)
  • Pip-Tazo EI: infuse over 4 hours
  • Meropenem EI: infuse over 3 hours
  • Caz-Avi: infuse over 2 hours
  • Colistin: infuse over 30–60 min
  • Ceftaroline: infuse over 60 min

No/Minimal Renal Adjustment

  • Linezolid — 600 mg q12h unchanged
  • Metronidazole — 500 mg q8h unchanged
  • Ceftriaxone — biliary excretion (safe in renal failure)
  • Clindamycin — hepatic metabolism
  • Doxycycline — fecal excretion
  • Azithromycin — biliary excretion

PK/PD at a Glance

  • Time-dependent (T>MIC): All β-lactams → Extended infusion
  • Concentration-dependent: Aminoglycosides, FQ → High single dose
  • AUC/MIC: Vancomycin (target 400–600) · FQ
  • Post-Antibiotic Effect: Aminoglycosides, FQ (prolonged) · β-lactams (minimal)
